# Source Notes

This skill distills a public Xiaohongshu post and visible selected comments about "不迂回立论 (No negative framing)" into reusable writing instructions.

## Main Principle

Avoid overusing "不是……而是" / "It is not X, but rather Y" as a default argumentative opener. Directly state what the thing is, instead of first describing what it is not, unless the contrast is necessary for reader understanding.

## Comment-Derived Heuristics

- Rebuild argumentative order without changing the core claim. Improve paragraph transitions and avoid mechanical repetition.
- Keep factual support verifiable. Do not add unverified information; mark uncertainty when needed.
- Prefer concise, complete sentences. Translate specialized terms into clear, accessible language when the audience is broader than the field.
- Add concrete details when prose is too empty: examples, data, method context, research observations, or specific mechanisms.
- Reduce AI-looking texture by avoiding automatic connector chains such as "首先 / 其次 / 然后 / 综上所述". Use natural transitions that match the logic.
- Mix sentence lengths and structures, but do not intentionally make prose sloppy. The goal is human clarity, not artificial imperfection.
- Use restrained qualifiers such as "在一定程度上", "from this perspective", or "in this context" only when they improve accuracy.

## Abstract Pattern

For academic abstracts, use a four-sentence inverted-pyramid pattern:

1. State background and knowledge gap in one sentence.
2. State the research goal or what the study did.
3. State the core finding directly and early.
4. State the implication or contribution.

The third sentence should carry the strongest finding. The first three sentences should already communicate the paper's central story.

## Quality Checks

- Does the revision begin with the claim rather than the negated alternative?
- Did any deleted contrast remove necessary precision?
- Are added details supported by the user's text or clearly marked as placeholders?
- Is the prose direct without becoming oversimplified?
- Does the final wording preserve the user's intended register, field, and language?
